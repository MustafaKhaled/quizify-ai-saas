# PMP Glossary

Stable term/definition pairs. Used by the agent to anchor terminology in questions
and by the frontend (future) as a study reference. Edit by hand — one concept per
entry, PMBOK-aligned wording.

---

## Baselines & Planning

**Baseline** — The approved version of a plan (scope, schedule, or cost) used as a
reference for comparing actual performance. Changes require formal change control.

**Scope Baseline** — Scope statement + WBS + WBS dictionary.

**Progressive Elaboration** — Iteratively refining plans as more information becomes
available. Applies most to planning early phases in broad terms and later phases in
detail.

**Rolling Wave Planning** — Planning near-term work in detail, distant work at a high
level. A form of progressive elaboration.

**Tailoring** — Adapting processes, inputs, tools, and outputs to fit the project
context. Every project tailors PMBOK; PMBOK is not a prescriptive checklist.

---

## Scope

**Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)** — Hierarchical decomposition of the total scope of
work. The lowest level is the **work package**.

**Scope Creep** — Uncontrolled expansion of scope without adjustments to time, cost,
and resources. Caused by weak change control.

**Gold Plating** — Team-initiated additions beyond requirements. The customer didn't
ask for them. Avoid.

**Validate Scope vs. Control Quality** — *Validate Scope* is formal customer
acceptance of deliverables; *Control Quality* is verifying deliverables meet
requirements before they go to the customer.

---

## Schedule

**Critical Path** — The longest path through the network diagram; determines the
shortest possible project duration. Activities on it have zero float.

**Float (Slack)** — The amount of time an activity can be delayed without delaying
the project (total float) or its successor (free float).

**Crashing** — Compressing the schedule by adding resources. Increases cost.

**Fast Tracking** — Compressing the schedule by running activities in parallel that
would normally be sequential. Increases risk.

## Cost & Earned Value

**BAC (Budget at Completion)** — The total approved budget.
**EV (Earned Value)** — Value of work actually completed, expressed in budget terms.
**PV (Planned Value)** — Value of work planned to be complete by this point.
**AC (Actual Cost)** — What was actually spent.
**EAC (Estimate at Completion)** — Forecast of total cost at completion.
**ETC (Estimate to Complete)** — Forecast of remaining cost.
**VAC (Variance at Completion)** — BAC − EAC.

See `formulas.md` for computation.

---

## Risk

**Risk Register** — Living document listing identified risks, owners, responses,
triggers.

**Contingency Reserve** — Time or budget for *known unknowns* (identified risks).
Part of the cost baseline.

**Management Reserve** — Time or budget for *unknown unknowns*. NOT in the cost
baseline; requires management approval to use.

**EMV (Expected Monetary Value)** — Probability × impact. Used in decision-tree
analysis.

---

## Team & Stakeholders

**RACI Matrix** — Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed. Maps people to
activities.

**RAM (Responsibility Assignment Matrix)** — Broader category; RACI is one form.

**Tuckman's Stages** — Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing, Adjourning.

**Stakeholder Register** — Stakeholders, their interests, influence, and engagement
levels (unaware → resistant → neutral → supportive → leading).

---

## Procurement

**Fixed Price (FP)** — Seller bears cost risk. Variants: FFP, FPIF, FPEPA.
**Cost-Reimbursable (CR)** — Buyer bears cost risk. Variants: CPFF, CPIF, CPAF.
**Time & Material (T&M)** — Hybrid. Common for staff augmentation.

**RFP / RFQ / IFB** — Request for Proposal / Quotation / Invitation for Bid.
