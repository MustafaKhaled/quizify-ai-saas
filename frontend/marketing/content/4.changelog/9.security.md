Three additions to round out the Goethe coverage:

**Goethe-A2 Hören**
Same architecture as B1 Hören but tuned for A2 — shorter scripts (50–70% the word count of B1), simpler vocabulary, fewer comprehension items per Teil, more replays per Teil (matching the real A2 exam where Teile 1, 3, and 4 play twice). A2 candidates can finally practice the format with vocabulary that matches their level instead of stretching into B1 to find any German listening practice at all.

**German Grammar B1**
The third grammar level lands. 17 chapters covering the full B1 syllabus — Konjunktiv II (komplett), Plusquamperfekt, Passiv (komplett), indirekte Rede (Konjunktiv I), Temporale / Finale / Konzessive Nebensätze, Konditionalsätze (real & irreal), advanced Relativsätze, N-Deklination, Genitiv (komplett), and more. The instructions explicitly tell the AI not to retest A1 or A2 mechanics — every question either tests new B1 grammar or chains B1 with earlier rules in a meaningful way.

**B1 Hören parallelized**
All four Teile now generate concurrently via a thread pool. End-to-end time drops from ~60–120s sequential to ~20–35s — comfortably inside reverse-proxy timeouts, faster feedback for the user, and identical output quality.
