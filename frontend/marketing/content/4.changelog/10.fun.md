The Lesen (reading) modules complete the Goethe coverage at A2 and B1 — and they ship with a brand-new question type the platform didn't support before.

**Goethe-B1 Lesen** (5 Teile, 30 questions, 65-minute timer)
- Teil 1: blog post + 6 richtig/falsch items
- Teil 2: two short press articles + 3 + 3 MCQ
- **Teil 3: 10-ad Anzeigen-Zuordnung — text input where you type the matching letter (a–j) or `0` for no match**
- Teil 4: 7 reader comments + Ja/Nein per author
- Teil 5: institutional document (Hausordnung, Mietvertrag-Auszug) + 4 MCQ

**Goethe-A2 Lesen** (4 Teile, 20 questions, 30-minute timer)
- Teil 1: short newspaper article + 5 MCQ
- Teil 2: building or department-store directory ("Wegweiser") + 5 MCQ asking which floor an item is on — unique to A2
- Teil 3: personal email + 5 MCQ
- **Teil 4: 6-ad matching + 5 situations — letter input (a–f) or `X` for no match**

**The new question type — letter_matching**

The Anzeigen-Zuordnung tasks needed a question shape we'd never built: the user types a single character into a text field, with soft-warn when they reuse a letter (each ad can match at most one situation). It's now a first-class question type in the scorer alongside MCQ, multiple-select, and true/false. The play page renders the ad pool in a sticky side column on desktop and a "↓ jump to questions" anchor on mobile, since the ads can't fit on screen alongside the inputs at smaller widths.

Per-level Lesen quotas: free trial gets 1 mock; Pro gets 10 per rolling 7 days across both A2 and B1.

This wraps the Goethe trifecta — Grammatik, Hören, and Lesen — at A2 and B1.
